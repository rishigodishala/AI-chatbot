The Big Five personality traits are Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism.

Openness: Refers to imagination, curiosity, and openness to new experiences. High scorers are creative and open-minded.

Conscientiousness: Involves organization, responsibility, and dependability. High scorers are disciplined and achievement-oriented.

Extraversion: Characterized by sociability, assertiveness, and energy. High scorers are outgoing and enthusiastic.

Agreeableness: Reflects compassion, cooperation, and trust. High scorers are kind and considerate.

Neuroticism: Indicates emotional stability or instability. High scorers experience more negative emotions like anxiety and anger.

These traits are used in recruitment to assess candidate fit for roles requiring specific behaviors.
